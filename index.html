Puerto Rican Asopao de Pollo

Not quite a soup and not quite a stew, asopao is a popular, hearty dish that can best be described as "Puerto Rican gumbo." Much like spaghetti sauce or chili, asopao is something that can be made in many ways, with different twists and "secret ingredients"-every cook has his or her own style of making it!
Since the Bronx is home to New York City's largest Puerto Rican population, there are a lot of unique, delicious asopaos simmering on stovepots alla cross the borough. This recipe uses chicken thighs and chicken sausage, but asopaos can also be made with seafood, pork, or gandules (pigeon peas).

  • Preparation time: 24 h
  • Cook time: 2 h
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients for Chicken Quantity
Onion powder 1 teaspoon
Garlic powder 1 teaspoon
Dried Oregano 1 teaspoon
Sweet Paprika 1/2 teaspoon
Ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon
tumeric 1/2 teaspoon
Coarse salt 1/2 teaspoon
Coarsely ground black pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into small chunks juice of 1 large ime (about 2 Tablespoons) 1 lb.
Ingredients for Asopao Quantity
Canola oil 2 tablespoons
1 medium onion, finely chopped 1 1/2 cups
1 small green pepper, finely chopped 1 cup
1 small red pepper, finely chopped 1 cup
2 cloves garlic, minced 1 heaping tablespoon
Dried oregano 1 1/2 teaspoons
Chicken or pork sausage, diced in small cubes 6 oz. 2 sausages
Diced tomatoes, including liquid 1 can (14 oz.)
Low-sodium chicken stock 1 quart (4 cups)
Cooked rice 2 to 3 cups

Preparation
First, prepare the chicken: In a bowl, combine all spices with salt and pepper. Mix well, add chicken, and coat with seasoning mixture. Add lime juice, mix well, cover, and chill in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

In a large Dutch oven or similar heavy pot, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add marinated chicken and cook, stirring regularly, until just cooked through. Remove chicken and set aside on a plate.

In the same pot, add onion and peppers. Saut√© until onion is soft and translucent. Add sausage and pan fry until golden. Add can of diced tomatoes and pour chicken stock over it. Add the rice and the remaining spices. Stir frequently and let simmer on low-medium heat until ready to serve.

Microwave Mac&Cheese

If you're a college student pulling an all nighter in need of an inexpensive meal, this is the food for you!

  • Cook time: 15 min
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients for Mac&Cheese Quantity
Kraft Macoroni and Cheese 1 box
Milk (fat percentage of your choosing) 1/3 cup
Butter (salt content of your choosing) 1 tablespoon

Preparation
First put the macoroni in a microwavable bowl. Add water until it just cover all the macoroni. Stir with metal fork and then place in microwave for 3 minutes on high, repeat this process 3 times remembering to stir frequently. Take bowl out of microwave and add 1/3 cup of milk 1 tablespoon of butter and cheese powder while continually mixing. Eat when all ingredients are mixed in fully.

Buttered Toast

If you need to make a quick breakfast, this is the food for you!

  • Cook time: 5 min
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients for Buttered Toast Quantity
Bread 1-2 slice (depending how hungry)
Butter (salt content of your choosing) 1 stick

Preparation
First slice the bread off the loaf. Place the bread in the toaster and hit the toast button. Pull bread out and spread butter across the bread until fully covered.
